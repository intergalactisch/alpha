# Personal Outcome Research

Status: research synthesis for the Personal Space seed field.

This document explores:

> What kind of personal outcomes are most Alpha-shaped?

It combines the current Alpha concept with research from motivation psychology, personal projects, personal informatics, goal pursuit, and calm technology.

It is not market research.

It is not product design.

It is a conceptual research pass.

## Current Alpha Frame

Plain-language north star:

> Alpha helps people shape life with care.

First seed field:

> Personal Space: Alpha first becomes visible as a private, care-bound environment where a person can make intent legible and shape an aligned, agreed-upon outcome in their world around them.

First Alpha loop:

> Personal Intent -> Contextual Clarification -> Aligned Understanding -> Agreed Outcome -> Fitting Form -> Cared-For Result.

The research question is not:

> What goals do people want to track?

The research question is:

> What kinds of personal outcomes reveal Alpha's loop without collapsing into self-improvement, productivity, tracking, or coaching?

## Research Anchors

### 1. Self-Determination Theory

Self-Determination Theory's Basic Psychological Needs Theory focuses on three deeply studied needs:

- autonomy
- competence
- relatedness

The official SDT overview describes basic psychological needs as essential for adjustment, integrity, and growth, and notes that need frustration increases risks such as passivity, ill-being, and defensiveness.

Alpha implication:

> Alpha-shaped outcomes should increase agency, felt capability, and healthy relation to others, not dependency on Alpha.

Source:

- [Basic Psychological Needs, selfdeterminationtheory.org](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/topics/application-basic-psychological-needs/)

### 2. Personal Projects

Brian Little's Personal Projects framework treats a personal project as a unit of analysis for personality in social, physical, and temporal context. The 1983 article describes personal projects through stages such as inception, planning, action, and termination, and relates projects to values, actions, and environment.

Alpha implication:

> The first Alpha outcome should probably be closer to a personal project than a task, habit, or goal. It should be situated in context, connected to values, and able to begin and end.

Source:

- [Personal Projects: A Rationale and Method for Investigation](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0013916583153002)

### 3. Self-Concordant Goals

The self-concordance model argues that people pursue goals more sustainably when goals align with their interests, values, and sense of self. Sheldon and Elliot's longitudinal model links self-concordant goal pursuit to effort, attainment, and well-being.

Alpha implication:

> The agreed outcome should not merely be useful. It should feel like it belongs to the person's own intent and becoming.

Sources:

- [Goal striving, need satisfaction, and longitudinal well-being: the self-concordance model](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10101878/)
- [New Empirical Support for Humanistic Theories](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2001_SheldonKasser_Goals.pdf)

### 4. Personal Informatics

The stage-based model of personal informatics describes systems through preparation, collection, integration, reflection, and action. This is useful because it shows that personal data systems often fail when they overemphasize collection without supporting meaningful reflection and action.

Alpha implication:

> Alpha should not begin with tracking. It should begin with intent and only collect or remember what the cared-for outcome actually needs.

Source:

- [Stage-Based Model of Personal Informatics Systems](https://personalinformatics.ianli.com/lab/model)

### 5. Lived Informatics

Research on "personal tracking as lived informatics" found that people interweave trackers in everyday life, use them socially as well as personally, and interpret tracking information through daily goals and decisions. The authors warn that personal informatics struggles if it ignores how tracking is enmeshed with everyday life and people's outlook on the future.

Alpha implication:

> Personal outcomes should be understood inside lived context, not reduced to metrics.

Source:

- [Personal tracking as lived informatics](https://researchportal.northumbria.ac.uk/en/publications/personal-tracking-as-lived-informatics/)

### 6. Implementation Intentions

Implementation intention research shows that goal intentions often fail unless people specify when, where, and how they will act. Gollwitzer and Sheeran's meta-analysis found a medium-to-large effect on goal attainment across 94 independent tests.

Alpha implication:

> Once an outcome is agreed, Alpha may help create a small fitting form that connects intent to action. But this should come after aligned understanding, not before.

Source:

- [Implementation intentions and goal achievement: a meta-analysis](https://www.socmot.uni-konstanz.de/publications/implementation-intentions-and-goal-achievement-meta-analysis-effects-and-processes)

### 7. Mental Contrasting With Implementation Intentions

Mental contrasting with implementation intentions, often associated with WOOP, combines a desired future with obstacles and an if-then plan. Meta-analytic work suggests this can support goal attainment.

Alpha implication:

> Alpha can help people hold both desired outcome and real obstacle, but it should avoid becoming a self-improvement script.

Source:

- [Meta-analysis of mental contrasting with implementation intentions](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8149892/)

### 8. Calm Technology

Weiser and Brown's calm technology work argues for technologies that move between periphery and center rather than constantly demanding attention. Their writing connects calm technology to a more humanly empowered ubiquitous computing future.

Alpha implication:

> Alpha's fitting forms should be transient and attention-respecting. A personal outcome should not create a permanent attention surface by default.

Source:

- [The Coming Age of Calm Technology](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology)

## Research Synthesis

The best first Alpha outcomes are not:

- goals
- tasks
- habits
- todos
- self-improvement plans
- metrics
- prompts
- purchases
- automations
- therapy-like conversations

Those can be ingredients, but they are not the shape.

The best first Alpha outcomes are:

> self-concordant personal projects in context, held lightly enough to evolve, concretely enough to shape, and carefully enough to protect agency.

That sounds academic, so the Alpha language can be simpler:

> personal intent becoming a cared-for outcome.

## Outcome Qualities

An Alpha-shaped personal outcome should have these qualities.

### 1. It Belongs To The Person

The outcome should feel connected to the person's own intent, values, season of life, taste, or becoming.

It should not be a generic "you should improve this" suggestion.

### 2. It Is Contextual

The outcome should make sense in relation to time, place, memory, mood, resources, constraints, and the person's world around them.

### 3. It Is Bounded

The outcome should be small enough to begin.

It should not expand into "manage my whole life."

### 4. It Is Livable

The outcome should not only be completed.

It should change the person's lived situation, even slightly.

### 5. It Can Become Form

The outcome should be able to appear as a fitting form:

- note
- ritual
- room change
- memory object
- small plan
- conversation preparation
- creative experiment
- decision aid
- quiet reminder
- generated temporary surface

### 6. It Can Be Cared For Over Time

The outcome should benefit from gentle continuity, but not constant monitoring.

### 7. It Can Fade

The form, memory, or support around the outcome should be able to disappear when no longer needed.

### 8. It Preserves Agency

The person should feel more capable afterward.

Not more managed.

Not more dependent.

Not more measured.

## Candidate Personal Outcome Types

### 1. Personal Transition

Example intent:

> Something in my life is changing, and I need to understand what shape it wants.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- deeply contextual
- tied to identity and becoming
- not reducible to task management
- can use transient forms
- benefits from care and restraint

Risk:

> collapses into life coaching or therapy cosplay.

Verdict:

> Very Alpha-shaped, but requires careful boundaries.

### 2. Creative Direction

Example intent:

> I want to make something, but I do not know what it is yet.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- begins from unclear intent
- supports agency and expression
- naturally produces transient forms
- can stay private before becoming cultural
- can avoid productivity framing

Risk:

> collapses into creator tool or generation machine.

Verdict:

> Strong first candidate.

### 3. Personal Environment

Example intent:

> I want the world around me to feel more aligned with who I am becoming.

This could involve a room, desk, home corner, morning setting, personal object, local place, or small atmosphere.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- literally shapes the world around the person
- connects intent to physical reality
- avoids pure screen/software framing
- can create a small, visible result
- can remain personal and bounded

Risk:

> collapses into shopping, interior design, or productivity setup.

Verdict:

> A strong expression because it makes "shape life with care" concrete in the person's immediate world.

### 4. Meaningful Conversation Preparation

Example intent:

> I need to have a conversation and want to approach it with care.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- intent, context, care, and agency are all present
- can remain private
- can improve real-world relationship without becoming communication app
- fitting form can be transient

Risk:

> collapses into message drafting or social scripting.

Verdict:

> Strong, but slightly more social than pure personal space.

### 5. Memory Lightness

Example intent:

> I want to preserve this memory without making it heavy.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- tests memory sovereignty
- continuity without total recall
- personal meaning over storage
- transparency and deletion matter

Risk:

> collapses into memory app.

Verdict:

> Deeply Alpha-shaped, but may become emotionally sensitive early.

### 6. Burden To Manageable Shape

Example intent:

> This thing is weighing on me. Help me make it manageable.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- high everyday relevance
- helps agency
- creates relief
- can produce small fitting forms
- avoids optimization if handled carefully

Risk:

> collapses into productivity, task management, or advice.

Verdict:

> Useful, but high collapse risk.

### 7. Personal Rhythm

Example intent:

> I want a small rhythm that supports who I am becoming.

Why this is Alpha-shaped:

- continuity over time
- care without constant monitoring
- can be screen-light
- connects daily life to becoming

Risk:

> collapses into habit tracker.

Verdict:

> Good later candidate; risky as first because habit/productivity gravity is strong.

## Ranking For First Exploration

Previous recommendation:

1. Personal Environment
2. Creative Direction
3. Personal Transition
4. Meaningful Conversation Preparation
5. Memory Lightness
6. Burden To Manageable Shape
7. Personal Rhythm

## Correction: Personal Direction Comes First

The Personal Environment recommendation was useful, but too narrow.

It made Alpha feel too physical and atmospheric too soon.

The stronger first field is:

> Personal Direction.

Personal Direction means:

> helping a person orchestrate their current goals, intentions, projects, transitions, and things they want to do into aligned, agreed, cared-for outcomes, under their own direction and in their own way.

Personal Environment remains important, but as one expression of Personal Direction.

Direction comes first.

Environment follows when it fits.

## Why Personal Environment Still Matters

Personal Environment may best reveal Alpha because it makes the north star tangible:

> Alpha helps people shape life with care.

It starts personal, but not purely internal.

It touches:

- physical space
- mood
- identity
- transition
- creativity
- memory
- objects
- atmosphere
- agency
- transient form

It can produce a small real-world outcome without becoming an app.

It can be bounded:

- one room
- one desk
- one wall
- one shelf
- one morning corner
- one object
- one sound/light/media atmosphere

It can avoid a lot of early traps if we define it carefully:

- not shopping
- not interior design
- not smart home automation
- not productivity setup
- not aesthetic optimization

The deeper outcome is:

> make a part of my world around me more aligned with what I mean, need, or am becoming.

## Updated Working Hypothesis

The first Alpha-shaped personal outcome may be:

> a person's current direction becoming clear, agreed, and cared-for enough to move in their own way.

Personal Environment is one possible expression of that:

> help me make this corner of my room feel like the life I am trying to enter.

That is not a product yet.

It is a research direction.

## Research Questions To Explore Next

1. What kinds of Personal Direction are most Alpha-shaped?
2. How can "orchestrating goals in life" feel warm, personal, and under the user's direction rather than managerial?
3. What language captures command without sounding cold?
4. Which expressions should Personal Direction support first: creative project, transition, burden, rhythm, environment, decision, or memory?
5. How can Alpha help current goals and intentions move without becoming productivity software?

## Current Recommendation

Explore **Personal Direction** first.

The reason:

> Personal Direction best captures the feeling that Alpha helps people orchestrate the things they want to do at this time, under their own direction, in their own way.

Personal Environment and Creative Direction remain strong expressions of Personal Direction, but they should not define the center too early.
