# MVP Options

The first MVP should prove that intent can become an adaptive lived experience without requiring the full future stack.

The MVP does not need robots, XR, universal agents, or perfect generated software. It needs to make one human or group feel:

> "I stated an outcome, and a living system began shaping reality around it."

These options are probes, not commitments. Alpha may begin with social life because the feeling is vivid, but the broader paradigm also includes business creation, economic participation, media consumption, information sensemaking, institutional navigation, and community governance.

## Candidate 1: Alpha Social Game Master

Audience:

- friend groups
- families
- Discord communities
- small teams
- creator communities

Intent:

> "Make this group feel more connected, playful, and alive."

What it does:

- creates a shared world or seasonal theme
- generates weekly missions
- coordinates plans
- creates rituals
- remembers inside jokes and history
- recaps moments as media
- assigns roles
- creates lightweight games
- helps turn chats into action

Why it is strong:

- emotionally resonant
- naturally social
- high shareability
- does not compete directly with productivity assistants
- can start as a chat-native product
- lets Alpha own "aliveness" rather than "efficiency"

Risk:

- groups are hard to activate
- novelty may fade
- needs excellent taste

## Candidate 2: Personal Life Quest Engine

Audience:

- ambitious individuals
- creators
- students
- self-improvement users
- people in transition

Intent:

> "Help me become this version of myself over the next 90 days."

What it does:

- turns goals into quests
- creates rituals and weekly arcs
- tracks behavior
- adapts plans
- generates tools and reflections
- coordinates with calendar, notes, fitness, purchases, and friends

Why it is strong:

- clear individual value
- easier onboarding than group products
- can become a personal operating layer

Risk:

- crowded productivity/self-improvement category
- can feel moralizing or exhausting
- may become another habit tracker

## Candidate 3: AI Experience Concierge

Audience:

- couples
- friend groups
- parents
- hosts
- travelers

Intent:

> "Create a memorable experience around this occasion."

What it does:

- plans events
- creates themes
- buys supplies
- generates invitations
- creates games and media
- coordinates schedules
- captures and recaps memories

Why it is strong:

- users already spend money on experiences
- concrete outcomes
- commerce model is natural

Risk:

- can collapse into event planning
- less persistent unless designed as ongoing experience memory

## Candidate 4: Creator Community World Engine

Audience:

- streamers
- newsletter writers
- educators
- fandom leaders
- indie game creators

Intent:

> "Turn my audience into a living participatory world."

What it does:

- generates quests
- creates lore
- manages community roles
- runs events
- produces recaps
- creates member challenges
- handles moderation support

Why it is strong:

- creators need differentiation
- communities already live in Discord/Slack/Circle
- economic model can attach to creator revenue

Risk:

- creator tooling is noisy
- requires distribution through communities
- moderation stakes are high

## Candidate 5: Intent Marketplace

Audience:

- consumers with complex needs
- service providers
- local businesses
- agents

Intent:

> "Here is what I want. Let the market fulfill it."

What it does:

- captures structured intent
- lets providers bid
- coordinates fulfillment
- verifies outcome quality

Why it is strong:

- massive economic upside
- replaces advertising pressure with intent-aligned fulfillment

Risk:

- marketplace cold start
- trust and fulfillment are hard
- too large for a first MVP

## Candidate 6: Business Genesis Engine

Audience:

- founders
- indie hackers
- creators
- small teams
- operators

Intent:

> "Turn this idea into a living business system."

What it does:

- researches markets
- defines positioning
- creates experiments
- generates landing pages and prototypes
- runs outreach
- tracks customer learning
- creates operating rituals
- builds lightweight internal tools
- coordinates agents, humans, and services

Why it is strong:

- high willingness to pay
- clear outcome orientation
- directly follows from AI coding and agentic work
- can become a business operating layer

Risk:

- crowded with startup tools and AI builders
- high execution expectations
- may pull Alpha back into app-building too early

## Candidate 7: Information And Media Sensemaking Layer

Audience:

- founders
- researchers
- investors
- creators
- knowledge workers
- intentional consumers

Intent:

> "Help me consume and understand information according to who I want to become and what I need to decide."

What it does:

- filters feeds
- summarizes sources
- compares narratives
- generates briefings
- converts information into actions
- creates intentional entertainment plans
- manages a personal or organizational knowledge graph

Why it is strong:

- daily use case
- broad market
- strong fit with multimodal AI
- direct alternative to engagement feeds

Risk:

- crowded with news, read-it-later, and research tools
- trust and source quality matter intensely
- can become another dashboard if not tied to action
